# Terminal scheduler bug — terminal_first_visit unreachable in production

## Context

Two playtesters in two days couldn't reach `terminal_first_visit` despite
playing builds where the gate flag write was already in place (04-17
cleanup migration). This is the most narratively-loaded scene in Arc One
(the terminal is the player's main investigation surface for the entire
frame story), and it was unreachable in production until this fix.

Original brief: `docs/CODE-BRIEF-2026-04-30-terminal-scheduler-bug.md`.

## Diagnosis (Phase 1-4)

The bug had two layers, both confirmed via `[STATE]` instrumentation
before any fix shipped:

**Layer 1 — sets_flag missing from DB:**
`glenn_pastime_paradise.choices[0].sets_flag` was missing in the live DB
despite the 04-17 migration explicitly patching it. The row's `updated_at`
was 2026-04-23, six days after the patch — something overwrote the field.
The clobber was specific to one row (terminal_first_visit's parallel patch
was intact). Without `sets_flag`, no `FLAG_SET` event was written to
`choice_log` on `head_to_evening` resolve, so `terminal_first_visit`'s
`requires_flag: glenn_gave_direction` predicate never matched.

**Layer 2 — frame-story crowded out by urgency cap:**
With Layer 1 fixed, instrumentation revealed `terminal_first_visit`
evaluated as eligible (`flagSatisfied: true, bestCandidate=terminal_first_visit`)
but was being dropped by the global `maxStorylets=2` cap because
life-stream tracks (belonging, academic) had storylets with shorter
expiry windows that won the urgency sort. Frame-story storylets have
14-day windows by design and are structurally unable to compete with
4–9-day life-stream slots.

## Resolution

**Layer 1 fix:** `supabase/migrations/20260430110000_restore_glenn_sets_flag.sql`
— idempotent restoration of `sets_flag` on `head_to_evening`.

**Layer 2 fix:** Frame-story slot reservation.
- `supabase/migrations/20260430130000_mark_opportunity_frame_story.sql`
  marks the opportunity track `category = 'frame_story'`.
- `src/core/tracks/selectTrackStorylets.ts` carves out one global slot
  for any frame-story track with an eligible candidate. Life-stream
  tracks compete for the remaining `maxStorylets - 1` slot(s). Total
  cap stays at 2 (default).

## Verification

- 4 new unit tests in `selectTrackStorylets.test.ts` cover slot reservation
  semantics, including the no-frame-eligible edge case (life-stream fills
  all slots)
- 2 new regression playthroughs:
  - `scripts/playthroughs/glenn_to_terminal.yaml` — earliest path (Day 0
    Glenn → Day 1 PM terminal)
  - `scripts/playthroughs/glenn_to_terminal_late.yaml` — delayed path
    (Day 2 Glenn → Day 3 PM terminal, mirroring the playtest capture)
- Three previously-passing tests updated to add a Day 1 PM
  terminal_first_visit resolution step (otherwise the fix's frame-story
  slot reservation kept terminal_first_visit perpetually surfaced and
  displaced their target storylets):
  - `scripts/playthroughs/week2_l1_job_board.yaml`
  - `scripts/playthroughs/week2_l3_first_shift.yaml`
  - `scripts/playthroughs/week2_l5_the_post.yaml`
- `npx tsc --noEmit` clean
- `vitest run`: 260 passed, 1 skipped (4 new tests added)
- `playthrough:all`: 16 passed, 6 failed, 22 total (vs baseline 12/8/20).
  All 6 remaining failures are pre-existing `first_morning` issues unrelated
  to this fix.
