# Collision Calendar — Arc One, Week 2

> Visual overlay of all streams competing for the player's time and attention
> across the second week. The guaranteed-slip principle: in every time slot, the
> player has more compelling options than they can pursue. Something always
> falls behind.
>
> Companion: `arc1-week2-brief.md` (full beat and landmark specifications).

---

## Calendar convention

- Day 7 = Monday, Day 13 = Sunday, Day 15 = Tuesday of following week
- Classes: Mon/Wed/Fri mornings and Tue/Thu mornings (assume ~9-11 class block)
- Time can slide between days — landmark *sequence* matters more than specific dates

## Stream legend

- **ROOM** — Roommate (Scott)
- **BEL** — Belonging (hallmates, Priya, Miguel, floor social)
- **ACA** — Academic (classes, papers, study)
- **MON** — Money (job track, bookstore residue, rent)
- **OPP** — Opportunity (ARPANET terminal, investigation)
- **HOME** — Home (family, Pat, Ohio ties)

## Guaranteed-Slip Summary

| Moment | What slips | Why it matters |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **Day 7 L1** | Three of four jobs | Locks the player's recurring weekly time drain for the arc |
| **Day 9 L2** | Evening activity if missed | The roommate relationship hardens without the player being present |
| **Day 11 Priya lunch** | The study group invitation (if missed) | Removes one of the four Tuesday pulls — invisible until Sunday |
| **Day 13 L4** | Three of four Tuesday activities | First four-way collision. The world moves without you in three directions |
| **Day 15 L5** | The seed thread (if any other Tuesday chosen) | Investigation delayed by at least a week; thread ages |

Plus a soft slip per day in the texture beats:
- Miguel's door is open Thursday, closed by Friday if ignored
- The pay phone line beat passes; Mom's next call has a quiet edge if skipped
- Doug's coach story is told once; if missed, Doug brings it up again but shorter
- Heller's pen-stopping moment is single-shot — miss class that day, miss it forever

## Anti-Optimization Audit

| Can the player... | Answer |
|-------------------|--------|
| Take a job AND preserve total flex time for investigation? | **No** — all jobs claim multiple slots/week |
| Be fully present for Scott's Thing AND attend the Wed library shift? | **No** |
| Attend Priya's lunch AND eat with Doug's group? | **No** |
| Get to L5 AND keep the study group going? | **No** — L4 is a terminal choice |
| Reply to `jstern` AND stay anonymous in the shared space? | **No** — reply is a commitment |
| Know which of the four Tuesday pulls is "best"? | **No** — all four lead to different Week 3 content; no dominant strategy |
| Recover L5 if missed in Week 2? | **Partially** — the thread ages but is still reachable in Week 3; the lead is cold |
| Optimize Belonging without paying in Academic or Opportunity? | **No** — all the texture beats trade small deposits against each other |

**Verdict:** Every major slot has a real cost. The guaranteed-slip principle holds.

## Notes for implementation

1. **Open slots are intentional.** Days 8/9/10/11/12/13 have "OPEN SLOT" markers in afternoon and evening. These are where the four held beats (Mike cracked door, Keith practical, Bryce invite, Scott phone call) would land — but we're holding on commissioning them until playtest feedback on the current seven.

2. **L5 is flagged as Day 15 here** for calendar clarity, but the brief explicitly says time can slide. Implementation can place L5 on whatever day makes the flow work; the brief's claim is that it fires on the *first Tuesday terminal window after L4 commits the player to it*.

3. **Class time is assumed attended.** No storylets fire during Mon/Wed/Fri morning class block except Heller's pen-stopping beat (Tue/Thu). Implementation can narrate class attendance as elided unless a specific class-time beat is commissioned.

4. **Stream pulls are approximate.** The 1-8 strength values are authorial guidance for which streams should dominate which slots, not hard numbers the engine reads. They help content writers choose what competes for each slot.

5. **Rebecca Chen appears in L3 if picked** but does not activate a new stream — she's a quiet character beat inside the MON stream that opens a door for Week 3 development. Whether she becomes a recurring presence is held for Week 3 design.
