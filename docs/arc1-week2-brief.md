# Arc One, Week 2 — Design Brief

> Companion documents: `mmv-lore-bible.md` (section 4.2 stub), `collision-calendar-week2.md` (slot-by-slot pulls and slips).

---

## Throughline

Week 2 is where the player's time starts belonging to other people. The week's arc is that the scarcity Week 1 only foreshadowed becomes concrete: a job commits weekly hours, a roommate crisis demands emotional presence, and by Sunday night four different things want the same Tuesday evening slot. The week closes with a forced choice that locks in what Week 3 will cost.

**Investigation enters the game this week** — not as solitary reading, but as shared conversation in the ARPANET terminal space where Knowers ask each other real questions about real things. The first seed thread (L5) is where the player learns they are not alone with the question.

## The five landmarks

### L1 — The Job Board
**When:** Monday Day 7 afternoon
**Where:** Student Union 204
**Type:** Terminal choice (locks recurring weekly time drain)

Corkboard with four real postings. The others are set dressing — babysitter in a faculty house, a card for a dog named Hutch, a dishwasher at Capri Pizza with six tabs already torn off.

The four that matter:
- **Library shelving**, Baker Library, evenings. $3.35/hr, 10 hrs/wk. Typed. Quiet. Has been up a while.
- **Dining hall**, breakfast shift. $3.35/hr, 15 hrs/wk. 5:30am Tue/Thu/Sat. Handwritten with a cheerful exclamation point.
- **Grounds crew**, maintenance. $4.10/hr, 12 hrs/wk. Outdoor, weather-dependent. Coffee ring on the card.
- **Research assistant, Economics Department**. $4.15/hr, 6 hrs/wk. "Data entry and source review. Some statistical background preferred." Typed on an IBM Selectric, clean. At the bottom, in pencil: *See R. Chen, Crandall Hall 304.*

The Chen posting is the cleanest card on the board. That's the only tell that it's unusual. A player who notices it and picks it is doing so because something about *source review* and the pencil addendum felt specific.

The choice locks the player's weekly time drain for the rest of the arc.

---

### L2 — Scott's Thing
**When:** Wednesday Day 9 evening
**Where:** Room 214
**Type:** FSM-gated three-variant scene (Roommate stream)

Scott gets a letter from home: his father has had a heart attack. The variant that fires depends on Week 1-2 roommate-track state.

**Variant A — `genuine_connection`** (player has built real deposits with Scott)
Scott looks up and says *hey.* After a beat: *my dad had a heart attack.* He says it flat because he's been holding it for two hours. The player can sit down, stand and listen, ask questions, or offer something practical. Eight-node conversational storylet. Scott doesn't cry. Talks about his dad's shop, the six-hour drive home, whether he should go. Asks the player what they'd do. Scene ends with Scott saying *I think I'm gonna try to sleep* and turning off the desk lamp while the player is still talking.

**Variant B — `surface_tension`** (player has been polite but distant)
Scott looks up, looks back down at the letter. The player can ask what's wrong, ignore it, or say *you okay* in the lowest-commitment way. If pushed, Scott says *my dad's in the hospital, it's fine, they think it's fine* and puts the letter in his desk drawer. The player can push once more and get shut down harder. Scott leaves the room for a long time. When he comes back the player is asleep or pretending to be.

**Variant C — `avoidance`** (player has been actively disengaged)
Scott isn't in the room. The letter is face-down on his desk. Envelope beside it, return address visible (Parma, OH). The player can look at the envelope, leave it alone, or read the letter (identity tag choice). Scott comes back around 2am. The player is asleep. In the morning Scott is gone before the player wakes up. A pay stub is on the player's desk with a note: *rent's due friday don't forget.* The note isn't angry. It's the note of someone who has decided the player is a logistics problem.

### L3 — The First Shift
**When:** Thursday Day 10 morning (or first-scheduled-shift of whichever job; see L1)
**Where:** Varies by L1 choice
**Type:** Four short variants, ~12 sentences each

- **Library variant.** Mrs. Doerr (librarian, 50s) explains the returns-cart system twice. Third floor smells like old carpet. Radiators click. Two hours later the player realizes they've shelved four hundred books without taking any of them in.
- **Dining hall variant.** 5:30am. Industrial dishwasher already running. Terry (40s, not a student) shows the player how to break down the egg station. At 7:15 Doug comes through the line, sees the player in the hairnet, doesn't make a joke about it. That's somehow worse than if he had.
- **Grounds variant.** Cold. The player is raking leaves on the north side of the library with a sophomore named Vince who doesn't want to talk. Vince says *you'll get used to it* without looking over. It's not clear what he means.
- **Chen variant.** Crandall Hall 304. Small office, two desks, window looking at a brick wall. Rebecca Chen (mid-20s, cardigan) studies the player for two seconds longer than is comfortable, then says *okay. Here's what we're doing.* Hands over a folder of *Journal of Monetary Economics* reprints and a yellow legal pad. *Mark anything about forward rate expectations. Don't summarize. Just mark the passages.* The player sits. Rebecca goes back to her own reading. Scene ends with the player forty minutes in, realizing Rebecca hasn't turned a page the whole time and is watching them work.

Rebecca is the quietest wrong-note of the week. She is not introduced as a Knower. She is not named as anything. She is a grad student who is *too still* while she watches a freshman work through photocopies. The player files it or doesn't.

### L4 — The Tuesday Commitment
**When:** Sunday Day 13 evening
**Where:** Room 214
**Type:** Reflection landmark + terminal choice

Not a dramatic scene. The player is in their room and realizes their Tuesday evening has four things wanting the same 8pm slot:

1. **Priya's Western Civ study group** (seeded by the Friday Day 11 dining hall beat) — academic and social value, meets Tuesday 8pm in Keith's room
2. **The ARPANET terminal** (seeded by Glenn's Week 1 directive, reinforced by Glenn's Walk on Day 13 of Week 1 if applicable) — Tuesday 8pm is the quietest window, Glenn mentioned it in passing
3. **Library shelving shift** (only if that job chosen at L1) — 8-10 Tuesday
4. **Scott's half-asked movie** (only if Scott's roommate-track state is high enough to warrant the ask) — *hey, you wanna maybe go see something Tuesday?*

The scene itself is short. Player sitting on the bed with the paper class schedule from their back pocket, mentally laying it out. No UI dashboard. No numbered options. Just four pulls visible in the player's head at the same time.

Player picks one. Terminal choice. The other three happen without them.

What makes this land is not the collision mechanics — it's that by Sunday night, all four pulls have texture from prior beats. Priya is real because of the grilled-cheese scene. Scott is real because of the dry cereal and the letter. The terminal is real because of Glenn's directive and the weight of the bleed. The library shift is real because the first shift was physical and specific.

### L5 — The Post
**When:** Tuesday Day 15 evening, 8pm (execution of L4 if terminal chosen)
**Where:** Whitmore Hall basement, ARPANET terminal
**Type:** Investigation landmark, conversational-node storylet

The player logs into the terminal. The room is empty. The CRT has the faint ozone smell of a machine that's been on all day. This isn't their first visit — the terminal is already a place they know how to be — so they skip the fumbling and start browsing.

They're not looking for anything specific. Glenn's directive was *find the others, work together*, and they've been carrying it around like a coin in a pocket without knowing where to spend it. So they browse. `net.politics`. `net.general`. `net.music`. Reading threads, scrolling through the residue of strangers.

In `net.politics`, buried in a thread about U.S.-Soviet relations, there is a post:

```
Newsgroups: net.politics
Subject: Soviet grain dependency — data sources?
From: jstern@[redacted]
Date: [three weeks before Day 15]

Trying to model current USSR hard currency constraints given
grain import levels since 1979. Anyone have access to CIA
unclassified series or Brookings projections on foreign exchange
reserves? Also interested in sustainability analysis on Western
Siberian oil — specifically whether peak production 1982-83 is
load-bearing or if there's slack.

Research project, not homework. Will share findings.

  - j.s.
```

Two replies, both 1983-normal: one recommending a 1982 Brookings report on Soviet grain trade, one asking what the research project is. `jstern` hasn't answered either.

**What makes the post a tell.** The question isn't impossible for a 1983 researcher. A serious Sovietologist could ask about grain dependency or hard currency reserves in 1983 — the data existed. The combination is the tell. Grain dependency PLUS hard currency reserves PLUS Siberian oil sustainability is a set of load-bearing questions about whether the Soviet economy can hold together. Soviet studies in 1983 almost uniformly assumed it could. Asking whether the oil is *load-bearing or if there's slack* implies the poster is already thinking in collapse scenarios. In 1983 that framing was fringe. In 1991 it was retrospectively obvious. The poster is asking the 1991 question in 1983, and they're asking in a way that suggests they already suspect the answer.

The player, if they're paying attention to the bleed, recognizes the wrongness. Not through any specific historical trivia — through the shape of the question itself. The Soviet Union in 1983 felt eternal. This post does not think so.

**Choices** (conversational-node storylet, ~6-7 nodes):

- **Reply with a contribution.** Draft something that adds to the thread — a source, a counter-question, a piece of information. This marks the player's presence in the shared space. Other players may read the reply. Terminal choice.
- **Lurk and read.** Read carefully, click through to other posts by `jstern` if the system allows. Full absorption, no commitment. The player will recognize the thread later if it reappears.
- **Bookmark and leave.** Note the thread, intend to return. Deferred.
- **Log off without acting.** Close it. The thread will age. The player's nerve may not return.

The scene ends with the CRT hum and the walk back across the dark quad toward Caldwell Hall. Scott is asleep when the player gets in. The player lies in bed and can't stop running the three sentences.

**What this landmark does:**
- Activates the `investigation_active` flag. Glenn's fourth directive is now concrete.
- Depending on choice, sets one of: `contacted_jstern`, `tracking_jstern`, `deferred_jstern`, `refused_jstern`.
- Seeds Week 3-4 follow-through. Whether `jstern` (or anyone else) replies in-game is decided later.

**What this landmark refuses to do:**
- Does not explain time travel, the collapse, or the endgame.
- Does not name `jstern`'s stance or goal. The poster could be anyone trying to do anything.
- Does not set any hidden tracker on the player.

`jstern` is not a unique NPC. There are many Knowers behaving like `jstern` on the shared terminal. Later weeks will seed additional threads with different voices. These personas will be developed as needed. For Week 2, `jstern` is the first.

**Tonal target for the landmark:** Disco Elysium for the text-on-screen reading; Carver for the framing (the empty basement, the CRT hum, the walk home, Scott asleep).

## The week's beats

Eleven beats interleaved with the landmarks. Each is 10-20 sentences, mostly prose with 2-3 micro-choices and small-or-no system effects. The beats carry the week's feeling; the landmarks carry its weight.

**Seven drafted:**

1. **The Pay Phone Line** (Mon Day 7 evening) — Two strangers ahead at the floor phone. One arguing with his mother, one reading a paperback. The receiver is warm when it's the player's turn. Three micro-choices: dial home, dial Pat, hang up.
2. **Scott, Cereal, 11pm** (Tue Day 8 night) — Scott eating dry Rice Krispies in the dark with a Tigers game on, offers the box.
3. **Heller's Lecture, Something Off** (Tue Day 8 morning) — Heller says something dry about primary group bonds. Tomás, grading at the side, stops his pen for three seconds. The player is the only one who notices. No micro-choice.
4. **Miguel's Guitar** (Thu Day 10 afternoon) — Miguel in his open doorway trying and failing to play the opening of Stairway. Keith's door is closed.
5. **Priya in the Dining Hall** (Fri Day 11 lunch) — Priya sits down uninvited with a library copy of *The Second Sex* and an unsolicited monologue about her sister's engagement. Invites the player to the Tuesday Western Civ study group. Seeds L4.
6. **Doug's Story About His Coach** (Sat Day 12 evening) — Common room, SNL on low. A long story with three false endings about a coach named Petrocelli.
7. **The Headline** (placement flexible — probably Fri or Sat) — Small divergence beat. The player sees a newspaper or hears a TV news item that almost-but-not-quite matches their memory. They can't tell which side is wrong. No micro-choice. Seeds the *testing memory against reality* mode of investigation small.

**Four more slots to commission after playtest feedback** (explicitly deferred):
- Mike studying with his door cracked (twelve seconds of scene, the crack is the point)
- Keith doing something practical (fixing a drawer, changing a bulb)
- Bryce party invite that doesn't require a response (sits on the player's desk as a mental object)
- Scott on the hallway phone with his mom, before the letter arrives

The decision to fill these four is held until we see how the current seven land.

---

## Tonal targets (week overall)

Mistry/Carver carries the beats. Specific physical detail doing the emotional work: the warm phone receiver from the guy ahead in line, Scott's dry Rice Krispies in the dark, Miguel's scratched guitar finish, Priya's library copy of Beauvoir, Doug's three false endings, Keith's closed door, Tomás's pen stopping for three seconds.

No named emotions. No evaluative one-liners. If the prose finds itself saying *the player felt*, rewrite.

L5 is the one Disco Elysium exception — text on a screen carrying ideas. Its framing (basement, CRT hum, walk home) stays Carver.

The week's throughline in one sentence: *time starts belonging to other people, and for the first time the player has evidence that somebody else is also carrying what they carry.*

## Resolved decisions

- **Multiplayer terminal infrastructure:** deferred to later implementation. Write seeds now, wire them up later.
- **`jstern` backstory:** deliberately minimal. One of many similar personas. Develop as needed.
- **Stance tracker:** dropped. No hidden counter. Faction alignment emerges organically from player history when factions are encountered in later arcs.
- **Scott vs Dana:** Scott. Earlier draft used "Dana" from a stale outline; this brief is canonical.
- **The Chen posting pencil addendum:** going to playtest as-drafted. Subtle is intentional.
- **L1's four options:** library / dining / grounds / Chen. Chen is the investigation-adjacent option; the other three are straight-up work with texture.
- **L5's technical-Week-3 placement:** L5 is Week 2's payoff. Tracked in Week 2 build. Day 15 designation is convention; time can slide.
- **Beat count:** 7 drafted, 4 held for playtest feedback. Do not commission the additional 4 until feedback arrives.

## Still open

2. **The `investigation_active` flag.** What other systems read it? Is it just a gate for Week 3 terminal content, or does it affect other NPCs' dialogue (e.g., does Glenn's Walk on Day 13 Week 1 change if the player already has the flag set)? Current assumption: Glenn's Walk is independent; the flag is purely forward-looking.
3. **Rebecca Chen.** She shows up in L3 as the `jstern`-adjacent quiet-watcher. Does she also appear in later weeks? Is she a Knower? Is she watching the player because she's watching *everyone* she hires (normal grad student paranoia) or because she saw something specific? Held for Week 3 design.
4. **The four commissioned beats.** Explicit hold until playtest feedback on the current seven.
